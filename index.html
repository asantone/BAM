Pet-Adoption Events and Stronger Registration Activity in Seattle

Municipal Pet Registration Trends, 2015 to 2018

Did the "Clear the Shelters" pet-adoption campaign
lead to a spike in mixed-breed dog registrations?

The fourth annual pet adoption campaign took place in Seattle, WA in August, 2018. This nationwide event encourages adoption of pets to help ‘clear the shelters’ and find good forever homes for pets in need. Pet registration records have been kept by the city of Seattle for over fifteen years and have proven to be a rich source of information regarding trends in ownership. From a recent analysis, figures from the national adoption campaign may be related to spikes in mixed-breed dog and cat registration.

Data from the adoption campaign and registrations may be indicate a trend. In September, the share of adopted mixed-breed dogs jumped significantly over July, increasing from 14% to 25% respectively. This also represents a big jump over three years ago, when the share of Mixed dogs was only 11%. With regard to the total volume of registrations, 816 mixed-breed dogs were adopted in August and September, an increase of 157% over the the same period last year.

The share of mixed-breed cats adopted in September 2018 also jumped significantly over June of that same year, increasing from 27% to 40%, respectively. This also represents a gain compared to three years prior, when the share was only 2%. In total, 1,677 mixed-breed cats were adopted in August and September, an increase of 45% over the the same period last year.
